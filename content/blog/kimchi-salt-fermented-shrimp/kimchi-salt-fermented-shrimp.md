## description

This salt-fermented shrimp recipe is much nicer than the store-bought versions, using only fresh shrimp and coarse sea salt to create a powerful seasoning base.

## ingredients

- 100 g Fresh Shrimp
- 40 g Coarse Sea Salt
- 1 Shrimp (with head-on)

## steps

### Main

1. Finely chop or pound the 100g of deveined shrimp until they form a coarse paste without large lumps.
2. In a small bowl, thoroughly mix the chopped shrimp with the salt. Stir for about 5 minutes until the mixture is well combined.
3. Spoon half of the shrimp-salt mixture into a clean glass jar and press down firmly.
4. Place the whole shrimp (head-on) in the center with the head facing upward to release its juices.
5. Cover with the remaining shrimp mixture, burying the whole shrimp completely, and pack it down gently.
6. Add a layer of coarse sea salt on top to act as a protective barrier.
7. Place a small piece of plastic wrap directly over the salt layer, then seal the lid tightly.

### Fermentation

1. Leave the jar on your kitchen counter at room temperature for one hour.
2. Move the jar to the back of the refrigerator (the coldest part) and let it ferment for at least a week before using.
3. The flavor will continue to improve over the first month. After a month of refrigeration, it should be moved to the freezer for long-term storage.
